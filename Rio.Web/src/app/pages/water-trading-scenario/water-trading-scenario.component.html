Water Trading Scenario

The Water Trading Platform can be integrated with the Groundwater Evaluation Toolbox (GET). By integrating these platforms, groundwater managers can quantitatively evaluate the impacts of water trades on regional groundwater levels. The map below shows a hypothetical water trading scenario which results in less groundwater usage in the vicinity of a mapped disadvantaged community (DAC), who relies on domestic wells as their source of water.

  • This scenario shows a trade occurring that provides a benefit to a nearby disadvantaged community (DAC).
  • The buyer is purchasing 600 acre-feet of water yearly from the seller .
  • The scenario assumes the purchased water is pumped uniformly throughout the year.
  • The water that the seller historically pumped, but will no longer, results in an increase in groundwater levels for the adjacent disadvantaged community (DAC) , who relies on private domestic wells for their drinking water.
  • Through a carefully managed trading program and associated analysis tools, the risks and benefits to all parties can be considered and managed.
