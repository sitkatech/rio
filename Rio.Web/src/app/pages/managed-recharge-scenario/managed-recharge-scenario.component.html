Managed Recharge Scenario

By integrating the Water Accounting and Trading Platform with the Groundwater Evaluation Toolbox (GET), groundwater managers can better understand the potential benefits and impacts of groundwater recharge activities.

The maps below present two options for recharging groundwater via district-owned recharge basins in either the western portion of the district (map on the left) or eastern portion (map on the right). The maps integrate data showing the locations of known groundwater quality contamination, nearby disadvantaged communities (DACs), and location of private domestic wells.

In this scenario:

  • Managed aquifer recharge options are evaluated at two different district-owned recharge basins .
  • In both options 500 acre-feet is recharged uniformly throughout the year.
  • Groundwater quality monitoring locations show areas where arsenic concentration exceed the regulatory limit of 10 micrograms per liter .
  • Changes to groundwater levels can also result in impacts to disadvantaged communities who rely on private domestic wells for their drinking water.

An enhanced understanding of the potential impacts and trade-offs of recharging groundwater in a given location can help groundwater managers and stakeholders make better management decisions. For example, recent studies describe how land sinking due to groundwater overpumping can release arsenic into aquifers, where it can then reach pumping wells. While other factors would likely need to be considered (e.g. other water quality concerns), in the scenarios shown below, it may be beneficial to recharge in the western recharge basin (map on the left), as increased water levels could help reduce arsenic contamination for disadvantaged communities reliant on private domestic wells for their drinking water.
